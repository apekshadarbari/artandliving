The Artist

My first creative
language was writing

In a land far, far away, over a quarter of a century ago, there was a little girl who always felt different, though she didn't know why. She started journaling at eight. Odd, considering no one in her family did. Following that instinct, not only was she discovering her love for writing but she was finding a channel — a safe space to say what she couldn't speak.

Once she left home for her engineering degree at 17, her writing practice was never quite the same again. For fifteen years in tech, the environments she moved through had use for output, delivery, performance. She was good at all of those things. So she got better at them, and quieter about everything else.

But she never stopped. Some part of her refused to let that channel close completely — pottery workshops after full days, woodworking courses, piano lessons. And then COVID removed those outlets too.

My life is to honour my nature,
To relish the fruit from my tree,
For that is all I have to offer,
To always be more of me.
My Art Today

A portal where there
used to be a wall

After her ADHD diagnosis and the work of reconnecting with her body, she got the itch to paint. A close friend offered her art supplies. They set up a paint date. And she created her first abstract piece.

It unlocked something — familiar and new at the same time. A simultaneous pouring out and filling up. Instinct as the whole method. No right answer, no performance to give, no correct way to show up.

My art practice is a commitment of unwavering support for my inner child and finding my way back to her. To that end, I will pursue whichever art form calls out to me. All the portals that need to open and all the cups that need to be poured and filled.

Why Therapeutic Arts

I was the woman who
carried too much.
Now I help others put it down.

Creativity is not a talent some people have. It is a human need. The particular relief I felt when I painted — the relief of being in a process with no right answer, no performance required — that is available to everyone.
